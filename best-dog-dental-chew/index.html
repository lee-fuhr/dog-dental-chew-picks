Quick answer

Greenies Original Dental Chews are the top pick for most dogs: they are widely recommended by veterinarians, have a flexible texture that scrubs teeth without being too hard, and come in sizes for small to large breeds.

For budget-conscious owners, Pedigree Dentastix offer a decent clean at a lower price; for puppies, the Nylabone Puppy Dental Chew Toy provides a safe first chew experience.

Dog dental chews are one of the easiest ways to support your pup’s oral health without nightly brushing battles. Chewing helps scrape away plaque and tartar while freshening breath, but not all treats are created equal. The key is finding a chew with the right texture, firm enough to clean but flexible enough to avoid broken teeth, and a shape that encourages contact with the gum line. You’ll also want to consider your dog’s size, chewing style, and any dietary sensitivities. How do you choose among the dozens of options? Start by matching the treat to your dog’s weight and jaw strength. A small chihuahua needs a different texture than a power-chewing lab. Some chews are trialled for plaque reduction and carry the Veterinary Oral Health Council (VOHC) seal; others focus on digestibility or natural ingredients. Below we break down five well-known picks, each with a clear strength and honest trade-off. No single chew fits every dog, but one of these should suit your pup’s needs.

Our verdict

No single dental chew works for every dog, but Greenies Original Dental Chews come closest to a universal recommendation thanks to their proven VOHC rating, flexible texture, and wide size range. If your dog has food sensitivities, the VeggieDent from Virbac is a worthy premium alternative. For owners on a tight budget, Pedigree Dentastix still offer decent cleaning at a fraction of the cost. Puppies and power chewers each have their own ideal pick, so match the chew to your dog’s age, jaw strength, and dietary needs. Pair any of these with regular veterinary dental checkups for the best results.
